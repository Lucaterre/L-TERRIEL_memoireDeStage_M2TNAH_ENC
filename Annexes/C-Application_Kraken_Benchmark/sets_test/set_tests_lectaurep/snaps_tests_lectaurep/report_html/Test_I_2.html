Kraken-Benchmark is a web application in the form of a metrics dashboard to assess the quality of your transcription model (model ML).

Kraken-Benchmark distinguishes between two types of metrics:


Evaluation of syntactic similarity

The metrics are based on classic algorithms for evaluating the syntactic similarity between two sequences of characters:


  • Levenshtein distance : also called editing distance, gives a measure of the difference between two strings. It constitutes the minimum cost to transform the reference sentence into the prediction sentence in terms of deletions, insertions or replacement of characters. The three transformation operations have a cost equal to 1.

  • Hamming distance : Close to the Levenshtein distance, with the difference that this metric only works on chains of the same length;

  • Word Error Rate (WER), Character Error Rate (CER), word accuracy : classic units of measurement for evaluating the performance of a character or word recognition system, who work from the distance of Levenshtein. These metrics give general indicators of the number of characters or words actually recognized. The lower the rate 0.0 the better the recognition, conversely the closer the rate to 1.0 the worse the recognition, for the word recognition rate, the higher the rate (close to 100%) the better the recognition;

as well as associated visualizations:

  • Histogram based on Ratcliff / Obershelp algorithm (Gestalt Pattern Matching) : evaluates the number of similar characters, deletions and insertions in character substrings common to the reference character sequence and the predicted character sequence by the model;

  • Versus Text : functionality which allows from a color code, to observe the transformations (common characters, deletions, insertions) between your reference text (ground truth) and your predicted text (hypothesis), this visualization is based on the Levenshtein's algorithm;

  • Confused pairs of characters ranking and confusion matrix : Visualize in the form of a ranking the most frequent pairs of character errors between the reference and the prediction (occurrences), this visualization is based on the Levenshtein algorithm;

  • Sequences in the form of signals : experimental visualization which aims to represent the sequences of characters in the form of signals from a dictionary which assigns a weight and / or a position to each of the known Latin glyphs in order to finely visualize the errors of types accentuation, capitalization and / or confusion of characters;



Evaluation of semantic similarity

Beyond the performance evaluation of a transcription model, Kraken-Benchmark also allows you to create an evaluation pipeline from the transcription of a corpus to your NLP projects (entity recognition, text mining, word spotting‚Ä¶) by proposing ‚Äúbag of words‚Äù type metrics (occurrences of words making up a given document) of semantic analysis with:

These metrics are based on normalization and text segmentation modules implemented in Kraken-Benchmark such as word and character tokenization and stop words removal.
